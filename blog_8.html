Safety Tips for Cleaning Around Pets

Giving our house or apartment a good cleaning not only makes us feel better, acting as a mood booster and de-stresser, but also a clean abode is physically healthier for us.     Use Pet-Friendly Cleaning Products:

  • Choose cleaning products that are labeled as pet-friendly or non-toxic. Harsh chemicals found in some cleaners can be harmful if ingested or if pets come into contact with freshly cleaned surfaces

    • Read Labels Carefully:

  • Always read the labels on cleaning products to understand their ingredients and any potential hazards they may pose to pets. Avoid using products containing ammonia, bleach, or other toxic substances.

    • Ventilate the Area:

  • When using cleaning products, ensure the area is well-ventilated. Open windows and doors to allow fresh air in and help dissipate any fumes that could be harmful to your pets.

    • Keep Pets Away During Cleaning:

  • While cleaning, consider keeping your pets in a separate room or area to prevent them from coming into direct contact with cleaning solutions or wet surfaces. This is especially important when using spray cleaners or floor cleaning solutions.

    • Secure Cleaning Supplies:

  • Store cleaning supplies securely in cabinets or areas that are not accessible to pets. This prevents accidental ingestion or exposure. Make sure lids are tightly sealed, and spills are promptly cleaned up.
